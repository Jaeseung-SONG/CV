I study how genetic variation shapes complex and polygenic phenotypes by integrating population genetics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and clinical data. I am particularly interested in tracing the regulatory pathways from genetic variants (genomics) through molecular mediators (transcriptomics, epigenomics, and proteomics) to phenotypes, while accounting for crosstalk among molecular modalities. My work focuses on analyzing multi-omics data and developing computational methods to resolve genetic effects at fine-grained scales.

Research interests

Dynamic and interaction QTLs

Mapping context-dependent regulatory effects across cell states and microenvironments using association models with interaction terms.

Polygenic disease

Connecting GWAS signals with gene and protein expression, epigenetic modifications, and gene regulatory networks to elucidate the pathogenic mechanisms underlying human polygenic diseases.

Multi-omics integration

Integrating bulk, single-cell, spatial, epigenomic, proteomic, and genotype data to generate rich, mechanistic insights into disease pathogenesis.

Biomedical AI

Developing predictive and generative models of responses to genetic and chemical perturbations to advance precision medicine.
