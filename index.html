Jaeseung Song, Ph.D.

Research Assistant Professor

Biomedical Research Institute
Seoul National University Hospital

I study how genetic variation shapes complex disease by integrating population genetics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and clinical data. My work focuses on computational methods that resolve genetic effects at cell-type and spatial scales.

Email ORCID GitHub

Research interests

Single-cell & spatial QTLs

Mapping regulatory effects across cell states and microenvironments, including PBMC eQTLs and serum pQTLs in migraine.

Polygenic disease

Connecting GWAS signals, transcriptome-wide associations, networks, and clinical phenotypes.

Multi-omics integration

Integrating bulk, single-cell, spatial, epigenomic, proteomic, and genotype data into interpretable models.

Biomedical AI

Developing predictive and generative models for drug response in single-cell transcriptomic spaces.
