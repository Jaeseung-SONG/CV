01 / Research

From variants to mechanisms—and, ultimately, better care.

My work integrates population genetics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and clinical data to explain how polygenic variation shapes disease. I develop computational methods that resolve these effects at cell-type and spatial scales.

01

Single-cell & spatial QTLs

Mapping regulatory effects across cell states and microenvironments, including PBMC eQTLs in migraine.

02

Polygenic disease models

Connecting GWAS signals, transcriptome-wide associations, networks, and clinical phenotypes.

03

Multi-omics integration

Combining bulk, single-cell, spatial, epigenomic, proteomic, and genotype data into interpretable models.

04

Biomedical AI

Building predictive and generative models for drug response in single-cell transcriptomic spaces.
